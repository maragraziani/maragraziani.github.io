AI interpretability

Hi! My name is Mara Graziani and I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Geneva. My career objective as a PhD student is to improve the trust of clinicians in machine learning systems for healthcare. There is still very limited work regarding the interpretability of automated diagnostic systems. Besides increasing trust and acceptance by physicians, interpreting machine learning systems could also illustrate aspects of the disease that were not known before. Read the latest paper on researchgate. I will present at SPIE2019-Medical Imaging the work in collaboration with the QTIM group at the Massachussets General Hospital (Boston, MA) on Improving Interpretability of computer-aided diagnosis of Rethinopathy of Prematurity.

I currently work on the EU Horizon2020 project PROCESS, developing exascale solutions for medical imaging together with top-level High Performance Computing infrastructures and big data centres in Europe. Formerly, I studied Machine Learning, Speech and Language Technology at the University of Cambrdige. My master thesis aimed at improving interpretability and generalization for deep learning on natural language processing tasks.
